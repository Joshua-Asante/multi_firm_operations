## F — O3 Conversion-funnel rolling drift

### F1. Striker conversion stages drift +154% to +166% over panel — **Forward → Closed (post-Q11)**

90-day rolling pass-rate at `session_pass` grew from 0.50% (early) to 1.27%
(late) — a +154% shift. `warmup_pass` grew +166%. `dow_pass` +44%, `body_pass`
+44%. The denominator is universe bars; the numerator is signals passing
each cumulative stage. Mechanically this means raw breakout signals
(`signal_raw`) are arriving more often inside the 13-17 UTC Tue/Fri window
late in the panel than early. Either more total signals are firing, or the
time-of-day distribution of signals has shifted toward NY morning hours.

**Q11** (cheapest-first): *"Decompose Striker `signal_raw` count
by hour-of-day and by 6-month period. If hour-of-day distribution is stable
and total count grew, the drift is volume-driven (more breakouts, evenly
distributed). If hour-of-day distribution shifted toward 13-17 UTC, the
drift is regime-driven (something is concentrating breakouts in the NY
morning window)."* Falsifiable from existing O3 rolling data + a small
re-aggregation; no Pepperstone needed.

**Q11 outcome (2026-04-26, first conscious Inquire-phase application of the
unified D-S-A pre-Q gate on this corpus) — REGIME-DRIVEN; F1 routes Closed.**

Pre-Q gate trace (in [`analysis/q11_striker_funnel_drift.py`](../q11_striker_funnel_drift.py)
docstring): D = dropped post-filter cohort (scope test, permitted §5);
S = compressed to hour×period pivot (preserves the shape question);
A = none needed (one pivot op, O(seconds)).

Result on OANDA US30USD 15min, 7,587 raw signals across 9 6-month periods
(2022-01-01 anchored):
- Total signals per period range 618–966 (P8 partial; per-month rate
  approximately stable at 130–177/month). **Volume is not the driver.**
- Fraction of signals in 13-17 UTC grew monotonically from **0.2043** (P0,
  2022 H1) to **0.2736** (P7, 2025 H2) and **0.2573** (P8, 2026 YTD partial).
  +7 percentage points absolute, +34% relative.
- KL divergence vs P0 hour-distribution grew monotonically from 0.000 to
  **0.098** at P8.
- Chi-square hour-distribution last vs first period: **stat 130.35, df 22,
  p < 10⁻⁶.** Highly significant shape shift.
- Top-3 hours per period: P0 = (08, 19, 07) → P7 = (13, 14, 06) → P8 =
  (14, 09, 11). Signal mass migrated from Asia/Europe-morning UTC hours
  into NY-morning UTC hours.

Routing: F1 → **Closed.** The funnel-stage drift observed in O3 is the
mechanical consequence of a real bar-level regime shift in US30USD breakout
hour distribution. Striker's locked filter (13-17 UTC, Tue+Fri) is in the
same window as the concentration, so the strategy mechanically passes more
signals through `session_pass` over time. The drift is structurally explained.

No operational implication for the locked Striker strategy: filter design
already restricts to the window the signal mass is concentrating into. No
parameter change motivated. None of the four Action rules trigger — no
Rule 0 read needed, no overlay candidate, no live-PnL gap, no documented
re-MC trigger fired. Closed under permitted "drift explained, locked design
absorbs it" routing.

## Post-synthesis a-priori closure of D1 / D2 / G1 — new permitted D-test

After Q11 / Q12 / Q14 / Q15 all routed Closed under the four Action rules
(see [synthesis page](https://www.notion.so/34edc0b53c118179b57cd38fc1f9f72e) — 2026-04-26, Joshua's
recommendation), the remaining three Forward findings (D1, D2, G1) are
closed a-priori without per-question Inquire runs.

### The new permitted D-test (declared, not silently substituted)

**Test name:** *Question-class extrapolation*.

**Statement:** A Forward question may be closed a-priori if **both** hold:
1. ≥4 questions of the same evidence-class have closed under the four
   Action rules within the same loop iteration, AND
2. The underlying observation (not the question framing) will be
   re-surfaced as a higher-leverage gated Q if residual operational
   tension persists.

**Why this is permitted (not forbidden):** The test does not encode the
conclusion the loop should reach. The underlying observation is preserved
in the Identify corpus (O1 / O4 outputs unchanged); only the question
framing is closed. If tension remains, it is *re-surfaced at the design
level*, where it should have been asked from the start. This is
compression at the question-class level, not signal deletion.

**Distinction from forbidden tests:**
- Not "Has been useful before?" (§5) — this is class-saturation in the
  current iteration, not history.
- Not "Doesn't fit my model?" (§5) — the underlying observation is
  preserved and re-routed, not deleted.
- Not "Signal is weak?" (§5) — applies to per-question framing, not
  signal magnitude.

**Why it's surfaced and not silently applied:** Per §5 — "If the test you
want to apply is not on the permitted list, stop, write the test, declare
it new, and surface it to Joshua before applying it." Joshua proposed the
extrapolation explicitly in this loop iteration (recommendation
2026-04-26, post-Q15-synthesis); this section logs the test for the
permitted-list addition record.

**Permitted-list addition (proposed):** *"Question-class extrapolation —
≥4 same-class closures within an iteration justify a-priori closure of
remaining same-class Q's, conditional on the underlying observation being
re-surfaced as a higher-leverage gated Q if residual tension persists."*
Pending review at the next gate audit.
