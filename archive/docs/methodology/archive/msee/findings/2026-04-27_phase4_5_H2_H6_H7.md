## Q-MSEE-7 — OOS regime forecast (H7) → CLOSED-MIXED

`analysis/msee/h7_regime_forecast.json`. Walk-forward logistic regression on 140 OOS weeks.

- **Regime forecast:** 75.0% hit rate (CI95 [67.4%, 81.6%]) vs 54.1% chance baseline → **WORKS**, regime predictability > chance. But recall is 96% on the dominant calm cluster (n=104) and 0–23% on the rare clusters (n=2, 12, 22) — model essentially predicts "calm" everywhere with high precision; can't resolve the rare regimes that actually matter.
- **Predicted-best strategy:** 35.0% top-pick rate (chance 33.3%) → **NOT supported.** Diagnostic: Guardian dominates allocation-weighted portfolio contribution in every cluster (mechanically — high R per trade × small allocation still beats Striker × medium allocation × medium R). Even after weighting by `risk_pct`, cluster→best map is `{0,1,2,3}: guardian`.

The honest reading: H7's "predicted-best strategy" framing assumes the optimal strategy *changes* by regime — but at the locked allocations, Guardian's contribution dominates uniformly. The storage-effect benefit is not strategy-rotation; it is **simultaneous operation with negative cross-covariance** (already shown in H3 / H6c). **Routes Closed-MIXED:** regime forecast works, but the strategy-rotation hypothesis behind H7 is the wrong frame for this allocation profile.
