# ADR: Guardian v5.1 architecture — pure trend-rider

**Date:** 2026-04-17
**Status:** Accepted
**Scope:** Guardian Gold strategy (XAUUSD 15min long-only)

## Context

Guardian v4 used active exit management: breakeven logic, trailing stop, MFE-based BE movement. These features are standard prop-firm-style additions to a trend strategy and were added to v4 on the intuition that they'd reduce drawdown and protect open profit.

CSV diagnostic on v4's trade history showed a different picture. A large share of winning trades were truncated by BE or trail management before the structural ATR-extension move completed. The strategy was leaving significant profit on the table to buy a smoother equity curve it didn't actually need — Guardian already had an acceptable DD profile, and the protection against giveback didn't offset the capped upside.

The question became: does exit management add any risk-adjusted value to Guardian, or is it pure subtraction?

## Decision

Strip all exit management. Guardian v5.1 enters on signal and exits only on:
- Stop loss (1.55 × ATR)
- Take profit (29 × ATR, far enough out to capture the full extension move)
- Grace stop (3-bar, 3 × ATR — protects against immediate adverse fill)
- maxHold (850 bars — timeout on stale positions)

No breakeven. No trailing stop. No MFE-based BE management.

## Alternatives considered

- **Keep BE/trail with tuned thresholds.** Multiple parameter sweeps on v4 attempted to find BE/trail settings that preserved upside. None reached v5.1's risk-adjusted returns. The exit-management complexity itself was the problem, not the parameter values.
- **Tighten trail instead of removing it.** Rejected — tight trails amplified the truncation problem; loose trails recovered most of v5.1's performance but with more code surface and no benefit.
- **BE only, no trail.** Rejected — MFE diagnostic showed BE triggering early on trades that subsequently retraced through BE before reaching TP. BE was not protecting profit; it was introducing a new loss mode.
- **Keep v4.** Rejected — measurable risk-adjusted underperformance vs v5.1 across the 4yr backtest.

## Consequences

Positive:
- 4yr backtest: Net $444,765, PF 3.17, RF 22.04, static DD 0%, max DD $20,182, μ/σ 1.54.
- Dramatically lower code complexity — fewer parameters, fewer interactions, fewer failure modes.
- Strategy behavior is legible: a reader of the Pine file can reconstruct the full decision graph in under a minute.
- Establishes the pattern later applied to the Striker v5 rebuild hypothesis.

Negative:
- Open positions will retrace farther than v4 before being cut. Individual losing trades will look worse in isolation.
- Psychologically harder to hold — watching a 20R winner pull back to 5R before hitting TP requires discipline the exit management previously handled.
- Max favorable excursion on trades that don't reach TP is fully surrendered.

Risks:
- If XAUUSD regime shifts away from sustained ATR-extension moves toward choppy mean-reversion, the no-trail architecture surrenders protection that may become relevant. Monitor regime indicators; if regime shift sustained, consider reintroducing limited trail.
- High per-trade variance. A small number of trades drive a large share of results. A losing streak of 5–10 trades is within normal distribution and must not prompt parameter intervention.
