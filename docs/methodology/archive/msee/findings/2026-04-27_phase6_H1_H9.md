## Q-MSEE-1 — Community matrix via MC perturbation (H1) → CLOSED-PARTIAL

`analysis/msee/h1_community_matrix.json`. ±10% per-strategy allocation grid; 7 runs (1 baseline + 6 perturbed) at 2K × 3 seeds.

### Structural finding

The strict Farmer community matrix `A_ij = ∂r_i/∂w_j` for `i≠j` is **identically 0 by construction** on this MC, because strategy daily P&L paths are independently bootstrapped from the panel. There is no within-MC mechanism for one strategy's allocation to affect another's daily P&L distribution. This is a property of the MC architecture, not an empirical finding about the strategies. Reporting as if `A_ij=0` were measured (vs structurally imposed) would be a methodological error.

### What is operationally meaningful: portfolio-outcome sensitivity

| Metric | ∂/∂w_guardian | ∂/∂w_striker | ∂/∂w_aegis |
|--------|---------------|--------------|------------|
| pass_rate | +1.96 | −0.08 | +1.00 |
| bust_rate | +2.70 | +1.58 | +0.56 |
| p99_dd    | +1.20 | +0.66 | +0.55 |

Read as: a 1pp (0.01 fraction) increase in Guardian allocation increases pass rate by ~2pp but also increases bust rate by ~2.7pp. This validates the lock-decision logic: Guardian is the highest-marginal-pass-rate AND highest-marginal-bust-rate strategy, so its allocation is the most consequential to tune. Striker is essentially pass-rate-neutral but bust-rate-positive — it provides timing diversification not pass-rate uplift. Aegis is intermediate.

**Routes Closed-PARTIAL** — strict H1 not testable on MC by structure; operational sensitivity matrix supports the locked-allocation logic. **No re-MC trigger fired.**

The two largest *operational* findings from MC perturbation:
- Bust-rate sensitivity to Guardian allocation is +2.70 — i.e., a hypothetical Guardian risk increase from 0.34% → 0.40% (+18%) would push bust rate from 0.65% (lock baseline) toward ~1.7%. This re-validates the 2026-04-23 risk re-lock (0.30% → 0.34%) being conservative.
