## Phase D — regime-robustness gate (load-bearing)

Adapted from canonical [`docs/methodology/regime_robustness_gate.md`](../regime_robustness_gate.md) (per-strategy trade-panel context; pattern from `docs/briefs/Q-DDP-1/_run_regime_robustness.py`).

**Setup:**
- 6-month time-window blocks: 9 blocks across the 52-month panel (sizes [20, 25, 23, 29, 20, 30, 16, 23, 11])
- Block bootstrap n=100 (seed 20260506)
- Half-panel split at trade_num 98: H1 = 86 base trades, H2 = 111 base trades

**Result for cap = 1.5R:**

| Test | Result | Threshold | Pass? |
| --- | ---:| ---:|:---:|
| Bootstrap p05 PF | 2.0347 | ≥ 0.95 × 2.8923 = **2.7477** | ❌ |
| H1↔H2 PF spread (relative) | 59.55% | ≤ **10%** | ❌ |
| Bootstrap p05 p99 DD | 4.30% | ≤ 5.19% + 0.5pp = 5.69% | ✅ |

**Two of three gate criteria fail decisively. Cap=1.5R is regime-fragile.**

Half-panel detail:

| Half | n | PF | p99 DD |
| --- | ---:| ---:| ---:|
| H1 (trade_num ≤ 98) | 86 | 1.8555 | 2.89% |
| H2 (trade_num > 98) | 111 | 3.4288 | 5.23% |

H1 is mostly 2022–2024; H2 is mostly 2024–2026 and contains the −5.94R / −$11,871 anchor trade #168 (2025-02-07). The cap's headline +24% PF improvement is essentially "fitting" to H2's worse loss distribution. H1's losses are mostly within 1.5R of the strategy's nominal R, so capping at 1.5R doesn't change H1 economics meaningfully.

This is structurally equivalent to the Q-DDP-1 C2 false-positive: full-panel single-pass passes; regime split reveals the win is concentrated in one half. The gate exists for exactly this scenario.

Bootstrap distribution: p05/p50/p95 PF = 2.03 / 2.83 / 3.74; p05/p95 p99 DD = 4.30% / 6.58%. The wide PF range (1.71 PF units across p05↔p95) reflects how few high-impact tail trades there are in the panel — bootstrap resamples that exclude the anchor block produce panel-PFs near the H1 baseline.

## What this verdict does and does not say

**Does say:**
- A hard dollar cap on DJ30 base-entry stops, at any sweep level surveyed (1.5R–3.5R), is not regime-robust on the locked Pepperstone 52-month panel.
- The worst-loss anchor (−$11,871 on 2025-02-07) is a real tail event, but it is one observation in a regime that the cap does not generalize across.
- Cap=1.5R produces +24% headline PF improvement; this is not transferable forward — bootstrap p05 PF of 2.03 is below the uncapped baseline PF of 2.33.
- Cap=2.0R came within 0.06pp of clearing Phase C; if a future re-fetch of the panel shows trade composition has shifted materially, 2.0R is the first cap to re-test.

**Does NOT say:**
- That all hard caps are bad in principle. The mechanism is plausible; it does not survive **this panel** under regime stress.
- That the −$11,871 trade is acceptable. It is the dominant tail observation. The verdict surfaces that the cap is the wrong instrument; the observation routes to Forward.
- That the load-bearing pyramid mechanism (42.7% of P&L) is at risk. Phase E was not run; that question remains open if a future cap formulation passes Phase D.
- That the 4-strategy 2026-05-05 MC anchor (97.88% pass / 0.22% bust / 4.55% p99 DD) needs revisiting. Verdict does not move policy.
