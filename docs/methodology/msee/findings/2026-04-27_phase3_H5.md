## Q-MSEE-5 — punctuated-equilibrium changepoint analysis → CLOSED-MIXED

`analysis/msee/h5_changepoint.json`. PELT (L2 cost) on rolling Sharpe of daily portfolio contribution (R × w on trade-days, 0 on non-trade business days). BIC-style penalty `2*sigma^2*log(n)`. Window sensitivity sweep.

### Window-dependence (decisive feature)

| Strategy | 30d window | 90d window (primary) | 180d window |
|----------|-----------|----------------------|-------------|
| guardian | 17 (drift) | 13 (drift) | 8 (transitional) |
| striker  | 22 (drift) | 7 (transitional) | 6 (transitional) |
| aegis    | 18 (drift) | 9 (transitional) | 5 (transitional) |

Verdict depends on the rolling window. Short windows (30d) detect natural Sharpe oscillation as "changepoints" and reject P5; longer windows (180d) smooth over noise and produce TRANSITIONAL counts. **No window produces a clean PUNCTUATED signature (<5 breaks/4yr) for any strategy.**

### What the segments actually show (at 90d, primary window)

- **Guardian:** alternates between Sharpe ≈ +1 to +3 (working) and Sharpe ≈ −2 to −4 (drawdown patches), with longer positive segments (199, 191, 115d) and shorter negative interruptions (15–47d). Pattern is "stationary positive edge with transient pullbacks" rather than discrete regime shifts.
- **Striker:** 7 changepoints with one extended Dec-2022→Sep-2023 negative segment (Sharpe −1.40, 190d) bracketed by sustained positive periods. This *is* the closest to punctuated structure in the panel — one clear regime "down" event followed by recovery.
- **Aegis:** 9 changepoints all positive-or-near-zero Sharpe; segments are roughly equal-magnitude noise around a long-run positive edge. No discrete regime shift detectable.

### Event alignment (±10 days)

- Guardian: 2023-10-10 ↔ Israel/Hamas (2023-10-07, +3d)
- Aegis: 2023-03-08 ↔ SVB collapse (2023-03-10, −2d)
- Striker: no alignment at 90d window

Two of 29 detected changepoints (~7%) align with the known-event list. With a 20-day window and ~8 known events on a 1031-day axis, baseline alignment rate ≈ 8 × 20 / 1031 ≈ 16%. Observed 7% is *below* baseline — i.e., changepoints are not preferentially event-driven. The few alignments that happen are at chance level.

### Routing decision

**CLOSED-MIXED.** The framework's P5 prediction (sparse punctuated structure) is not supported at common rolling-Sharpe windows. The honest finding: detectable regime shifts exist but they are not the rare-event signature Eldredge-Gould describes — closer to "noisy stationary edge with occasional medium-duration pullbacks."

This **does not falsify the underlying biological metaphor**, but it does reject the specific operational prediction P5 as written. Constructive reframing: the signature *might* exist on a different metric (cumulative R slope, rolling profit factor, regime-conditional Sharpe), and on a different time scale (rolling 1yr+) — both are out of scope for this iteration.

### What it does NOT trigger

- No re-MC trigger (rolling-Sharpe pattern is independent of bust-rate / pass-rate metrics).
- No allocation change.
- No overlay (would require live-PnL gap, which doesn't exist).

The Striker Dec-2022→Sep-2023 negative segment is the highest-information artefact; it survives sensitivity analysis (visible at 30d, 90d, 180d). It is logged for the Phase 8 watch-list as a precedent for "what a Striker drawdown event looks like."
